## TLDR

- **Enterprise AI pricing is quietly shifting from seats to outcomes.** Vendors are testing usage-based tiers as buyers push back on per-seat sprawl, [reported across several outlets](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/enterprise-ai-pricing).
- **Foundation-model margins are the new battleground.** Inference cost curves, not training runs, now decide who can afford to compete, per [a widely cited analyst note](https://www.bain.com/insights/ai-inference-economics).
- **Regulators are circling usage data.** The FTC signaled interest in how AI vendors meter and bill, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/technology/ftc-ai-billing-2026).
- **Open-weight models keep eating the low end.** Cheap, good-enough models are compressing the price umbrella for incumbents.
- **Procurement teams are getting sophisticated.** Buyers now model token spend the way they once modeled cloud commit.

## enterprise AI pricing

- **The per-seat model is cracking under its own logic.** When one "seat" can do the work of ten, charging per human stops making sense — and vendors know it. The Information [details a wave of pricing experiments](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/enterprise-ai-pricing) where outcome-based tiers quietly replace seat counts, while [Bloomberg notes](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/ai-seat-pricing) that buyers are increasingly refusing to sign seat-based renewals. The awkward part nobody says out loud: usage-based billing makes revenue forecasting a small act of fortune-telling.
- **Inference economics now set the floor on price.** Margins live and die on cost-per-token, and [a McKinsey analysis](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/ai-inference) argues the winners will be whoever industrializes inference, not whoever trains the biggest model — a tidy reversal of the 2023 consensus.

## regulatory landscape

- **Washington discovers AI has a billing department.** The FTC has started asking how vendors meter usage, a line of inquiry [Reuters first surfaced](https://www.reuters.com/technology/ftc-ai-billing-2026), with [Axios adding](https://www.axios.com/2026/ftc-ai-metering) that opaque token accounting is the specific worry. It is a very FTC move to discover the meter before discovering the product.
